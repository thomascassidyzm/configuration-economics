# Proposal for evaluation — SSi as the local-currency delivery surface

A candidate artefact for the DNA-economy implementation arm, written as the input to a six-hat evaluation. Idea-stage; many claims are proposal, not fact, and are flagged for the White hat.

## The proposal

SaySomethingin (SSi, saysomethingin.com) is a Welsh company running a digital Welsh-language learning app with a global learner base and real political capital around Welsh-language survival (aligned with Welsh Government's Cymraeg 2050 strategy). The proposal: **SSi's app becomes the trusted delivery + ledger + legitimacy surface for a local credit/currency system** serving a DNA-economy cell (e.g. Ogwen / Gwynedd) — issuing and recording credits that people earn for value-creating activity and spend locally.

## Why it could be powerful (the CE framing)

A living language is a vast option-space — a reachable set of meaning, relationship and identity — and its loss is the asymmetry at its purest: fast to lose, generations to rebuild, irreversible at the community level. So preserving Welsh is a no-regret / viable-objective move by definition. But the market does not price language-preservation — it is **frozen capability: real Δω that nobody is paid to produce.** A local currency lets a community pay for the Δω-positive work the market ignores — language, care, retrofit, repair — and SSi is the surface that makes such a credit issuable and spendable.

Two further fits with the framework:
- Running credit through SSi's own surface **routes around the financial chokepoint** (no bank or payments giant required) — the move named in `route-around-the-chokepoint`.
- As a values-aligned Welsh company rather than a private fintech, SSi has a genuine shot at the **collective-ownership / less-to-none gate** that separates abundance-for-all from secession.

## Earn mechanisms — two markets, one surface

- **Diaspora / learners (global):** time-in-app learning Welsh → credits. Plausibly a grant-backed **reward** currency (Cymraeg-2050-aligned funding pays to incentivise learning).
- **Heartland (Ogwen / Gwynedd — mostly native speakers, so learning-Welsh does *not* fit here):** earn = energy-warden hours, retrofit, care, local services → a Δω-backed **value** currency.

These are different objects (reward vs value currency) with different backing. The proposal must not conflate them — one surface, two layers.

## Company-as-exemplar

Beyond mechanics: SSi would demonstrate a local company building abundance infrastructure for its own community — a tasteable example of local sovereignty that enrols others (Fuller: the exemplar enrols more than the argument).

## The sharp questions for evaluation

1. Can SSi issue a credit that is (a) genuinely **backed**, (b) **spendable** on real local value, (c) **below the regulatory line** (a points/rewards scheme vs e-money + taxable income / benefit-in-kind), and (d) does **not** just inflate into worthless points?
2. Is the **two-market split** (grant-backed learner reward vs Δω-backed local value) coherent on one surface, or does it pull SSi in two directions?
3. Is **"company-as-exemplar of local sovereignty"** a real strategic asset, or a distraction from SSi's core language-teaching mission and business?

## Confidence note

SSi as a Welsh-language learning company is web-verifiable. Its internal capabilities (whether the app could host a ledger, user numbers, governance, the founder's relationship to it) and any financial/regulatory specifics are proposal-not-fact — for White to flag and check where possible.
