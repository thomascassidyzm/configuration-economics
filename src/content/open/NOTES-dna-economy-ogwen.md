## The sequence — each loop unlocks the next (the upregulation)

**Turn 0 — the seed (exists).** Ynni Ogwen hydro. What it has already banked is not the ~100kW — it's the *capability*: the community knows how to co-own and run generation, has the legal vehicle and a value-capture fund, and has proven trust. That accumulated pattern-intelligence + governance is what lowers the action-cost of every turn below. *This is why Ogwen beats Caernarfon as the first cell: turn one is free.*

**Turn 1 — widen the energy loop (lowest action).** Replicate the proven co-op model: more micro-hydro on the valley's many high-rainfall streams, aggregated rooftop solar, demand-shifting. Enabled by: the existing model + falling solar cost + Welsh-Gov community-energy support. Funded by: community shares + the existing fund + grants. **Unlocks:** a genuine energy *surplus* and a revenue stream large enough to start paying local human time. **Gate:** passes — but run-of-river touches river ecology, so honest ecological care is the "less to none" test (binding-constraint). **Wall [hard, current]:** grid *export* connection capacity in rural N Wales is a real, slow, expensive bottleneck — the configuration is buildable but the grid operator holds the gate.

**Turn 2 — the heat loop (the big leak).** Heat is most of domestic demand in cold wet Wales, and it's almost all *imported* — so closing it retains the most value. **Honest correction to the geothermal idea:** Gwynedd's hard slate/igneous geology is *wrong* for the deep-aquifer geothermal that heats those Austrian towns (those sit on hydrothermal sedimentary basins). But there's a locally-specific substitute: **mine-water heat** — the flooded slate workings hold a large, stable ~12–20°C water body that heat pumps can lift to district-heat temperature (proven in UK coal country — Gateshead, Seaham; slate workings are a candidate [VERIFY suitability of Penrhyn workings]). So: mine-water + heat pumps run on Turn-1 electricity → a district heat network. This literally *thaws a frozen asset* (a drowned quarry) into a heat loop — maximally CE. **Unlocks:** the oil/LPG import leak plugged → large retained local value → funds Turn 3. **Walls [hard]:** district-heat networks are capex-heavy; and retrofitting solid-stone slate terraces to hold heat is a real materials+capital wall. **Gate:** passes strongly; honest edge — heat pumps and retrofit materials are *imported atoms* (matter doesn't yield to local currency).

**Turn 3 — the food loop.** Now there's cheap local energy + waste heat. Controlled-environment growing (greenhouse/vertical, the Netherlands move) on the surplus + waste heat, alongside existing upland livestock and the coast (fish/aquaculture nearby). Close the nutrient sub-loop: food/farm waste → anaerobic digestion → biogas (back to energy/heat) + digestate (fertiliser). **Unlocks:** food-import leak reduced; a nutrient cycle that feeds back into Turns 1–2. **Gate:** passes — honest edge: *not* full food autarky day one (closed nutrient/seed inputs are a later turn); claim food *resilience*, upregulating toward self-sufficiency.

**Turn 4+ — the making loop (Fuller's tools→meta-tools, anchored).** With energy/heat/food value retained, freed capital + the local currency fund local *making*: repair, fabrication, retrofit materials — plausibly using the **slate-waste mountains** as a feedstock (aggregate, construction, slate products). Powered by surplus energy. **Unlocks:** import-substitution of goods, more local human time employed, more skills banked. **Wall [honest]:** many goods (panels, heat pumps, chips) need global supply chains — you won't make them in Bethesda. Name what stays imported; don't pretend otherwise.

Build on what exists (the Ynni Ogwen community fund) toward a local mutual-credit / time-credit that **pays local human time to build the loops and recirculates the value created.** Backed by real Δω each loop produces (kWh, kWh-of-heat, kg-of-food, £-of-import-avoided). ## What upregulates each turn (the DNA term)

Each loop the cell accumulates four compounding things, and *these* — not the atoms — are what make turn N+1 cheaper than turn N: (1) **skills/know-how**, (2) **governance + trust** (coordination-wealth), (3) **freed capital + currency velocity**, (4) **legibility** — data about its own flows, so it can see the next leak. That compounding is the upregulation; it's `configuration-generates-configuration` localised, and pattern-intelligence finding the next-lowest-action loop.

## The walls, in red (where time/tech/currency do NOT help)

- **Grid export capacity** — held by the DNO/National Grid; current, hard, not yours to fix quickly.
- **Capital hump** — heat networks + solid-stone retrofit; currency mobilises labour, not the up-front matter.
- **Legislative power** — mandating new-build standards or running a heat network needs powers held at county/Welsh-Gov/national level. This is `coordination-bounds-reachability` exactly: the configuration is buildable but held by whoever holds the planning/legislative power. The cell must lobby up or route via voluntary/incentive paths.
- **Imported atoms** — panels, pumps, CEA kit, chips: energy self-sufficiency ≠ material self-sufficiency (binding-constraint / substitution-limits).
- **Spoilers** — heating-oil incumbents, absentee/second-home landlords, planning objectors, grid inertia. Positive-sum design enrols the many; route around the few; never expect unanimity.
