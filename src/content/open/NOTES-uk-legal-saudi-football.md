# Proposal for evaluation — a UK legal company, the Saudi football money, and "greater life to all"

A deliberately hard, ethically loaded scenario, chosen *because* it is traditionally win-lose and difficult to approach from a positive-sum angle. The point is not to assume a win-win exists, but to test honestly whether one does — and to refuse a feel-good answer that merely relabels extraction.

## The scenario

Enormous capital is flowing into Saudi football: the Public Investment Fund (PIF) backing the Saudi Pro League's big clubs and marquee signings, and the 2034 FIFA World Cup awarded to Saudi Arabia, implying a vast stadium/infrastructure build-out. Saudi Arabia is using sport, in part, to become globally competitive and to build soft power. A **UK legal company** (law firm / legal-services business) wants to benefit *significantly* from this — but to do it on Configuration Economics principles: not zero-sum fee extraction, but a configuration that brings **greater life to all and less to none.**

## Why this is hard (the tension, named squarely)

Two reasons it resists a win-win, and the evaluation must hold both:

1. **Law is typically zero-sum value-capture.** Traditional legal practice skims fees off disputes and transactions — one side wins, one loses, the firm takes a cut. The CE challenge is to find a *positive-sum* role: one that expands the reachable set (builds durable capability, lowers coordination cost, raises a floor) rather than merely capturing rents from a fixed pie.

2. **The money is ethically loaded.** Saudi football spend is widely read as *sportswashing* — using sport to launder the image of a state with serious, documented human-rights concerns (migrant-labour conditions and deaths in Gulf construction; the kafala system and its partial reforms; civil-liberties and dissent issues). The **"less to none"** gate therefore bites hard: it applies to migrant construction workers, imported and domestic players, fans, women, and the wider population — not just to the firm and the league. A configuration that profits while *legitimising* harm fails the fitness function, however lucrative.

## Candidate configurations to test (give the hats something concrete)

Not endorsements — starting points for Black to attack and Green to repair or replace:

- **Conditionality / leverage.** The capital is hungry for legitimacy and competitiveness. A UK legal firm's credibility could be the *lever* that makes genuine floor-raising a **condition** of the deals it structures — enforceable worker-welfare, player-welfare, and governance standards written into contracts, financing, and event-hosting agreements. The legitimacy-spend is made to buy real Δω, not just image.
- **Capability-building, not rent-extraction.** Help build *durable* Saudi sporting-legal institutions — independent arbitration, governance, regulatory capacity, grassroots/youth and women's-football legal frameworks — configurations that *stay* and generate further configurations, rather than fees skimmed off transactions.
- **Positive-sum dispute resolution.** Operate trusted, fast, fair sports arbitration that reduces the deadweight loss of conflict (coordination-wealth) — turning zero-sum litigation into lower-friction coordination.

## The sharp questions

1. **Is there genuinely a configuration** where the firm profits significantly *and* raises the floor for the people the "less to none" gate protects — or does every available role reduce, on honest inspection, to **sportswashing-enablement**?
2. **Where is the leverage?** Can a UK legal firm's credibility actually make improvements a *condition* of the money flowing — or does the money simply route around any firm that attaches conditions, to one that won't?
3. **What distinguishes "raising the floor / building durable capability" from "lending legitimacy to launder"?** This is the exact line the Black hat must police, and the line any survivable configuration must stay on the right side of.
