## Page intent

This page exists because the main essay is calm and the citations are quiet. An informed reader, finishing the essay, has a right to ask *who said what already, and what is actually new here*. The lineage page is where that question gets a serious answer.

It also serves the living-epistemic-work format. Every proposition in this work carries an epistemic status; the framework as a whole owes the same accounting. The page separates the credibility apparatus from the essay proper, so the essay can stay invitational while readers who want the receipts can find them.

It is not a bibliography. It is a structured statement of debts: who established what, what this work inherits, and where the specific delta lies. Where the delta is thin, the predecessor stays as predecessor — there is no need to overclaim novelty against a thinker whose work this one largely follows.

## CE's specific claim

Configuration Economics argues that economic value, properly accounted, is the maintenance and extension of configurations that keep the reachable set of life-supporting futures open under bounded exergy flux. Value is not throughput, not preference, not labour-hours, not aggregated welfare. It is what a move does to `R_living(C, B, T)` — the configurations reachable from the current state, under available exergy, over relevant horizons, in which living entities continue to exist and make distinctions. The unit of evaluation is the local change in that reachable set, `Δω`, not a global scalar over states. That move — configuration-under-exergy as primitive, observer-relative reachable set as object, local `Δω` as decision rule, no-regret preservation as objective — is what the rest of this page situates against its predecessors.

## Predecessors by cluster

Seven clusters. Each opens with one sentence on what the cluster contributes; each predecessor paragraph names what they argued, what CE inherits, and where CE's specific delta lies.

### 1. Physical grounding

*Where the energy-and-entropy register entered economics, and where the strong-sustainability case was built.*

**Frederick Soddy — *Wealth, Virtual Wealth and Debt* (1926).** Soddy was a Nobel-winning chemist who argued, decades before anyone else did it seriously, that money and physical wealth are different things and that conflating them is the root of recurring economic crisis. He named the divergence between monetary signal and physical referent as an active source of dysfunction, not a curiosity. CE inherits the energy-accounting move itself; `energy-income-inheritance` and `money-as-signal` are direct intellectual descendants. CE's delta is the forward-looking value frame — Soddy was diagnosing the error, not proposing an alternative accounting. He showed that the signal had drifted; CE proposes what the signal should have been tracking.

**Nicholas Georgescu-Roegen — *The Entropy Law and the Economic Process* (1971).** The foundational work of thermodynamic economics. Georgescu-Roegen argued that the economic process is fundamentally dissipative, that entropy is the correct accounting unit, and that neoclassical growth theory violates physical law. CE inherits throughput-as-cost (`throughput-cost`), physical foundationalism, and the entire register of the argument. CE's delta is on the value side: Georgescu-Roegen's framework is rigorously diagnostic about dissipation; CE attempts a positive measure — what the configurations actually do for future possibility. `exergy-not-energy` tightens Georgescu-Roegen's "low entropy" into the more precise "exergy," and the chess-moves frame (`option-space-as-chess-moves`) gives the diagnostic a decision procedure he never claimed to provide.

**Herman Daly — *Steady-State Economics* (1977), *For the Common Good* (1989, with Cobb), and four decades of ecological-economics work.** Daly made Georgescu-Roegen's argument concrete: scale, distribution, and allocation as three distinct problems; critique of Solow on substitutability; steady-state as alternative to growth. CE inherits the `substitution-limits` argument, the critique of treating physical and financial capital as interchangeable, and the ecological-economics baseline that natural capital is not freely substitutable by manufactured capital. CE's delta is the move from steady-state-as-target to no-regret-over-moves (`viable-objective`). Daly constrains throughput at a level; CE evaluates configuration changes by their effect on the reachable set. The two frames are compatible — CE's stance entails throughput restraint in most regimes — but the decision procedure differs.

### 2. Configuration as primitive

*Where configuration itself became an object of analysis: how arrangements enable further arrangements, how complexity is measured, how capability grows over time. Split into two sub-rooms — the first names the structural mechanism, the second names how the mechanism shows up as economic dynamics.*

#### 2a. Configuration space dynamics

*The local-mechanism case for how configuration enables configuration, and the structural-physics case for what that implies about time itself.*

**Stuart Kauffman — *The Origins of Order* (1993), *Investigations* (2000), and the "adjacent possible" concept.** Kauffman named the local mechanism by which configuration enables configuration. Each new arrangement makes nearby new arrangements reachable that were not reachable before; the space of the possible is not given in advance but generated by what already exists. He developed this for biology — auto-catalytic sets, the origin of order — but the move generalises. CE inherits the adjacent-possible intuition directly: `R_living(C, B, T)` is, in spirit, the adjacent possible restricted to life-persisting observers and bounded by an explicit exergy budget. CE's delta is the restriction itself — the observer-class and the budget make the object accountable in a way Kauffman's more discursive treatment does not require. Kauffman is the conceptual ancestor; CE adds the constraints that turn it into an accounting frame.

**Sara Walker, Lee Cronin, and the Assembly Theory programme — Assembly Index (early 2020s), Sharma et al., *Nature* 2023, Walker's *Life as No One Knows It* (2024).** Cronin's Assembly Index began as a backward-looking measure of molecular complexity — the minimum number of construction steps required to produce an object — and CE has cited it since 2025 as exactly that. The 2023 *Nature* paper and Walker's 2024 book reframe assembly forward: the universe's trajectory through configuration space is, on their reading, what time itself is; what exists is constrained by what has been assembled, and what becomes possible is constrained by what now exists. This closes, from their side, the gap CE had been citing — that Assembly Theory was backward-only and CE needed a separate forward measure. The honest update is that Assembly Theory has been doing more of CE's work than CE was crediting; the framings are closer than the 2025 draft suggested. CE's delta in the current state of the art is the observer-class restriction (`R_living` rather than reachable configurations *simpliciter*), the exergy-budget and horizon parameters as accounting primitives, and the move-evaluation decision procedure on top of the state-space description. CE and Assembly Theory now overlap more than they diverge; the relationship is collaborative rather than corrective. The v0.4 generative-side proposition (in draft) treats Walker and Cronin as a load-bearing structural predecessor and Kauffman as conceptual ancestor.

#### 2b. Capability accumulation

*Where the configuration runaway shows up as economic and technological dynamics — capability per unit input, combinatorial technology, evolutionary economy.*

**Buckminster Fuller — *Operating Manual for Spaceship Earth* (1969), *Critical Path* (1981), and the concept of ephemeralisation.** Fuller's "doing more with less" is the operational case of the configuration runaway. Capability per unit input grows over time because accumulated configuration — tools, methods, knowledge, design templates — lowers the cost of new configuration. A modern car carries more capability per kilogram than a 1920s car carried per ton; the gain is not in the matter but in the arrangement. CE inherits Fuller's instinct that the right register for productivity is capability-per-input, not output-per-hour, and that the trend is configurational. CE's delta is the constraint side. Fuller was congenitally optimistic about how far ephemeralisation could run; CE pairs the configuration-runaway with the binding constraints (`binding-constraint`) — sinks, specific materials, biosphere integrity — that govern which configurations remain stable at all. Fuller saw the gain; CE accepts the gain and adds the floor.

**W. Brian Arthur — *Increasing Returns and Path Dependence in the Economy* (1994), *The Nature of Technology* (2009).** Arthur's work on increasing returns, path dependence, and combinatorial technology gave economics a non-equilibrium register and a story for how technology actually evolves: existing components combine to form new components, which then become available for further combination. CE inherits the combinatorial-and-path-dependent framing and treats Arthur's technology-as-recombination as a special case of configuration-enables-configuration. CE's delta is the physical substrate. Arthur grounds the combinatorial dynamics in technology and economic interaction; CE locates them in the broader configuration space including bodies, ecosystems, institutions, and infrastructures, with exergy as the explicit budget (`configuration-not-information`, blocks the easy slide to pure-information abstraction).

**Eric Beinhocker — *The Origin of Wealth* (2006).** Beinhocker's synthesis of complexity economics for a general audience framed wealth as encoded design / fit-to-environment information, accumulating through evolutionary processes of variation, selection, and replication. CE inherits the evolutionary, non-equilibrium framing and the claim that wealth is structural rather than monetary. CE's delta is again on substrate. Beinhocker's "fit design" tends toward an algorithmic or informational reading; CE insists that configuration includes physical maintenance, embodied skill, and ecological relation, and that none of these reduces to information that could be copied at near-zero marginal cost (`configuration-not-information`).

### 3. Complexity, resilience, and breakdown

*Where the trajectory of complex systems through time — accumulation, maintenance overhead, regime shift, collapse — became analytically tractable.*

**Joseph Tainter — *The Collapse of Complex Societies* (1988).** Tainter's thesis: complexity has diminishing marginal returns. Societies invest in complexity to solve problems; returns decline; eventually maintenance cost exceeds benefit and the system simplifies abruptly. CE inherits Tainter's complexity-maintenance argument near-directly (`complexity-maintenance`). CE's delta is the framing. Tainter is historical-comparative and predicts *when* systems break; CE locates the same phenomenon inside an option-space frame and asks *which configurations are option-space-preserving in the first place*. Note honestly: Tainter's thesis is contested in historiography; CE inherits the structural insight without committing to the universality of the historical pattern.

**C. S. Holling — adaptive cycle and panarchy work (1973 onwards).** Holling's adaptive cycle (exploitation, conservation, release, reorganisation) and panarchy (cross-scale interactions among cycles) gave ecology a way to talk about systems that are stable through change rather than stable in place. CE inherits the stability-as-adaptation framing (`stability-not-stasis`) and the cross-scale interaction picture that polychess later dramatises. CE's delta is the observer-relative restriction. Holling's adaptive cycle is observer-neutral — the cycle runs whether or not a particular class of life persists; CE evaluates moves specifically against `R_living`, which makes some adaptive cycles option-space-positive and others not, even where Holling's framework treats them symmetrically.

**Brian Walker and David Salt — *Resilience Thinking* (2006).** Walker and Salt operationalised Holling's resilience concept for management practice: maintain the system's capacity to absorb disturbance without flipping into an alternate regime, and recognise when a flip has occurred. CE inherits the threshold-and-regime register, which sits behind `transition-fragility` and `asymmetry-of-option-space-change`. CE's delta is the asymmetry argument made explicit. Walker and Salt name the thresholds; CE argues that breakdown is structurally faster than build-up, and that this asymmetry is what makes the no-regret rule operationally tighter than expected-value optimisation. Resilience thinking diagnoses the regime shift; CE prices it.

### 4. Labour, allocation, and what economies actually do

*Where economics was challenged on what it counts, who it counts, and what it ignores.*

**Karl Polanyi — *The Great Transformation* (1944).** Polanyi argued that labour, land, and money are not produced for sale and therefore are not really commodities; treating them as such required a violent reorganisation of pre-market society. The "commodity fiction" is his central move. CE inherits the recognition that labour's market form is historically unusual and structurally strained. CE's delta is the analytical step Polanyi did not take: labour was functioning as an allocation mechanism for access to life-supporting configurations under scarcity, not as the source of the value those configurations carry (`labour-as-allocator`). Polanyi makes labour's strangeness as a commodity legible; CE supplies the specific allocator-vs-source distinction.

**David Graeber — *Debt: The First 5,000 Years* (2011), *Bullshit Jobs* (2018).** *Debt* documents that wage labour is historically unusual; most of human economic life ran on credit, obligation, gift, and kinship rather than on hours-for-coin exchange. *Bullshit Jobs* observes that a substantial fraction of contemporary paid work fails the practitioner's own usefulness test. CE inherits both: Graeber supplies the historical texture for `labour-as-allocator` (wage labour as one allocator among several) and the empirical symptom (work-as-access-ticket) that `labour-as-allocator` explains as expected output of an allocator retained after the regime it was allocating in has shifted. CE's delta is the accounting frame Graeber did not articulate. His framing is anthropological and political; CE locates the same phenomena inside a configuration-and-allocation grammar that says *why* the symptoms appear when they appear.

**Marshall Sahlins — *Stone Age Economics* (1972), and the substantivist tradition.** Sahlins's argument that "the economy" in pre-market societies is embedded in kinship, ritual, and ecological relation — and that allocation of life-supporting goods happens via mechanisms other than wage labour — gives the historical record for `labour-as-allocator`. CE inherits the evidence base. CE's delta is interpretive: where Sahlins makes the substantivist case against universalising market economics, CE makes the structural case that any allocation mechanism (wage, kinship, command, ritual) can be analysed by what it allocates and how well its allocator tracks the configuration-contribution it is allocating against.

**Marilyn Waring — *If Women Counted* / *Counting for Nothing* (1988); Nancy Folbre — work on care and the invisible economy.** Waring demolished GDP's treatment of unpaid care; Folbre has extended the argument for decades. Feminist economics as a tradition has argued that the most economically essential activities are the ones most invisible to standard metrics. CE inherits this critique directly: `care-as-configuration` is a consequence of taking this tradition seriously, and `participation-limits` echoes the unconditional-participation argument. CE's delta is the physical grounding. Where feminist economics has often made the case morally and politically — *we should value care* — CE attempts to ground it configurationally: care is maintenance of arrangements that preserve `R_living`, and the moral claim follows from the structural one rather than carrying it.

**James C. Scott — *Seeing Like a State* (1998).** Scott named the active power of legibility. States and large institutions can only act on what they have made legible to themselves, and the process of making things legible can destroy the practical (metis) knowledge that made the thing work in the first place. High-modernist schemes — collectivised agriculture, planned cities, scientific forestry — fail in characteristic ways for this reason. CE inherits Scott's argument near-directly: `legibility-truth-tradeoff` names the same phenomenon at the economic-accounting layer. CE's delta is the placement inside the option-space frame — legibility-induced loss is one of the ways option space contracts under bureaucratic action, and the tradeoff has a price standard accounting cannot see. Scott is the historical-political case for the structural argument CE makes; CE renders it in terms of `R_living` and `Δω` rather than in terms of state-power as such.

### 5. Decision under deep uncertainty

*Where the practical question — how to act when you cannot compute the optimum — got rigorous treatment.*

**Dave Snowden — Cynefin and complexity-management practice (1999–present).** Snowden's distinction between complex and merely complicated problem domains, and his insistence that complex domains demand entirely different practice — probe-sense-respond on the next-best-move rather than analyse-plan-execute on the optimal solution — is the operational ancestor of CE's chess-moves frame. CE inherits the local move-evaluation stance directly (`option-space-as-chess-moves`); Snowden's "vector measurement" (where + direction of change) sits behind `Δω`'s structure as a directional rather than scalar object. CE's delta is the physical grounding of the vector. Snowden is deliberately anti-quantification in the complex domain; CE retains a forward-looking measurement aspiration (option space as research programme) and narrows to the life-persisting observer class where Cynefin is generic across complex domains. Snowden's frame is methodological — how to act; CE attempts ontological — what value is. Registers compatible; aspirations diverge. Note that Snowden's reception in formal academia is mixed; cite him alongside Aubin so the lineage carries both registers.

**Jean-Pierre Aubin — *Viability Theory* (1991, and subsequent volumes).** Aubin gave a rigorous mathematical formulation of admissible trajectories under non-empty constraint sets — under what conditions can a system stay inside a constraint envelope indefinitely, and which trajectories preserve that capacity? CE inherits Aubin's apparatus as the mathematical anchor for the no-regret form of `viable-objective`: preserve R_living non-empty for every major class of life over every relevant horizon. CE's delta is what gets to be in the constraint set. Aubin's viability is a general framework; CE specifies that the constraint is structural persistence of life-as-category under explicit exergy and horizon parameters, and adds the asymmetry argument (`asymmetry-of-option-space-change`) as a structural reason to prefer viability-preservation over expected-value maximisation.

### 6. Scoping and growth limits

*Where the empirical case against unbounded growth, and the framing of operating spaces and boundaries, got built.*

**Jason Hickel — *Less Is More* (2020), and the wider degrowth literature.** Hickel makes the empirical case against absolute decoupling of GDP from material throughput at the scale and pace required by planetary boundaries, and argues that economic coordination must be rethought to function within them. CE inherits the growth-skepticism under physical constraint and the seriousness of absolute (not merely relative) decoupling as the question. CE's delta is structural rather than polemical. Degrowth literature is policy-oriented and prescriptive; CE is descriptive, focused on configuration quality rather than on prescribed contraction. Throughput goes down in the CE view in most regimes — but the primary move is redirecting what counts as value, not prescribing *less*. CE is not degrowth-in-disguise; the difference is real and worth naming.

**Tim Jackson — *Prosperity Without Growth* (2009, second edition 2017).** Jackson develops the case that prosperity does not require GDP growth and articulates a macroeconomics of post-growth as a research programme. CE inherits the post-growth framing and Jackson's careful distinction between prosperity (capability for flourishing) and output (GDP). CE's delta is the substrate. Jackson's prosperity is welfarist; CE's is configurational — the question is which arrangements maintain `R_living`, and the welfare framing emerges as one consequence among several.

**Kate Raworth — *Doughnut Economics* (2017).** Raworth's doughnut frames a safe and just operating space as the annular region between a social floor (basic needs) and an ecological ceiling (planetary boundaries). CE inherits the two-sided constraint framing and the visualisation discipline of an explicit operating envelope. CE's delta is the structural object inside the envelope. The doughnut is a static map of where to operate; CE's `R_living(C, B, T)` is a dynamic object — the reachable set evolves with each move — and `Δω` is the local rule that says which moves keep the doughnut's interior reachable for future generations rather than just inhabited now.

### 7. Foundational meta-theory

*The author's own prior work, included honestly so readers can see where the vocabulary comes from.*

**Distinction Physics (Cassidy, 2025).** DP is the foundational meta-theory in which distinctions are primitive, distinction-making costs energy, and observers have finite distinction-making budgets. Configuration Economics is the 2026 application: configurations are patterns of distinctions, and configurations expand or contract future distinction-making potential. Per the v0.2 direction (April 2026), CE's main argument now stands on non-equilibrium thermodynamics plus ecological economics; DP is offered as preferred foundational vocabulary, not required grounding. The variational bridge from DP §4.2 is worked out as one optional formalisation of option space. The lineage page is one of the few places this self-citation is appropriate. CE's delta over DP is one of register — DP is the physics; CE is the economic accounting that the physics motivates — not of substance.

## What CE adds that none of the above gives

A short list. Each item is a specific move; together they are the framework's contribution.

- **Observer-relative `R_living`** as the object of evaluation. The "for whom" question gets a structural answer (life-persisting observer class, after Wolfram's *Observers Like Us*) rather than a utilitarian aggregation or an unmarked anthropocentrism (`observer-relative-option-space`).
- **Local `Δω` on chess-moves** as the decision procedure. Snowden's next-best-move stance, grounded in physics and tagged with an explicit exergy budget and horizon, applied to `R_living` rather than to generic complex domains (`option-space-as-chess-moves`).
- **Asymmetry as structural justification of no-regret.** `asymmetry-of-option-space-change` names why no-regret is not conservatism by temperament but the operationally tighter rule under the build-vs-break time-scale ratio; this gives `viable-objective` its structural ground rather than its ethical one.
- **Configuration, not information.** `configuration-not-information` blocks the easy slide from "value is in arrangement" to "everything can be digitised"; this is what keeps CE from collapsing into a software-flavoured complexity economics.
- **Labour as allocator, not value-source** (`labour-as-allocator`). The distinction between coordination machinery and the thing being coordinated, articulated as an accounting move rather than a political one.
- **The living-epistemic-work format itself.** Propositions carry explicit status markers (established / derived / contested / open); the work as a whole carries a version history; predecessors are mapped openly. The format is part of the contribution, not a presentation choice.

## A note on Distinction Physics

DP is offered as preferred foundational vocabulary, not required grounding. A reader who finds DP useful gets a tighter version of CE's claims; a reader who does not can read CE on non-equilibrium thermodynamics and ecological economics alone and lose little. The §4.2 variational bridge to option space is available, optional, and structurally compatible with the rest of the framework. CE survives if DP drifts in formulation, because the economic argument was rebuilt in v0.2 to stand on its own physical base.

This self-citation is included here, not in the essay, because the essay should not have to argue for its own foundational vocabulary. The lineage page is the appropriate place to be explicit.

## A note on novelty

CE is not claiming to be without predecessors. The format requires marking what was inherited and what is new; this page is part of the framework's epistemic discipline, not a claim to originality. Most of CE's individual moves have ancestors. The specific contribution is the assembly — observer-relative reachable set, local move-evaluation, structural asymmetry, configuration grammar, living-epistemic format — and the discipline of saying so where each move comes from. Several of the predecessors above are doing more of CE's work than CE was crediting in earlier drafts (Walker and Cronin in particular); the page updates that honestly. Where the delta is thin, the page says so.

The strongest form of the novelty attack — *Soddy 1926, Georgescu-Roegen 1971, Daly 1977, Tainter 1988, Holling, Kauffman, Fuller, Hickel — what is new here?* — gets a calm answer: observer-relative `R_living`, local `Δω` on chess-moves, the asymmetry-as-structural-justification, configuration-not-information, labour-as-allocator, and the living-epistemic format. Everything else is inheritance, and the work owes it openly.

## Proposed page structure (`/lineage.astro`)

Render shape, not Astro code:

- Header band matching `/research-frontier`: title (*Lineage and Positioning*), subtitle (*who said what already, and where this work tries to add*), small note (*this page is the credibility apparatus; the essay is calm because this page is doing the heavy work*).
- One short orienting block carrying the *CE's specific claim* paragraph above.
- Seven cluster blocks, each with: cluster name as `<h2>`, one-line "what this cluster contributes" italic note, then predecessor entries as a vertical list with left-border accent (mirror the `frontier-item` shape from `/research-frontier`). Each predecessor entry: name + work in bold, dates in italic, paragraph, **delta line** rendered as a separate visual element (think `item-open` block from `/research-frontier`, with a label like *CE's delta* and the closing sentence). The delta visual is what makes the page legible at scan-speed.
- A *What CE adds* block: a tight list, same styling as the `item-predictive` boxes on `/research-frontier`, one short line per move.
- A *Note on Distinction Physics* block: lighter weight, faded border, one paragraph.
- A *Note on novelty* block: same lighter weight.
- Footer band matching `/research-frontier`: links back to overview, essay, explore, applications/moves, research-frontier.

Cluster headers want enough vertical breathing room to feel like sections, not subheadings. Predecessor entries want the left-border treatment that `frontier-item` uses, so a reader can scan by cluster and dive into specific entries. The delta block per predecessor wants to be visually distinct — that is the page's load-bearing element. Aim for the same overall density as `/research-frontier`; the page is long but should read as scannable rather than essayistic.

Status badge in nav (analogous to version/build): possibly a "lineage" indicator counting predecessors, or omit. Decide on promotion.

## Editorial decisions made

The five open questions surfaced in the agent-drafted v0 are now resolved:

1. **Cluster sub-split** (2026-05-18): *Configuration as primitive* split into 2a *Configuration space dynamics* (Kauffman, Walker/Cronin) and 2b *Capability accumulation* (Fuller, Arthur, Beinhocker). Sub-rooms inside one cluster rather than two top-level clusters.
2. **Walker / Cronin tone**: kept as written — collaborative rather than corrective. The honest concession ("Assembly Theory has been doing more of CE's work than CE was crediting") is what the living-epistemic format is for.
3. **Taleb inclusion** (2026-05-18): dropped. Intellectual convergence is partial; brand-association cost exceeds lineage benefit for a public-facing page. Canonical asymmetry proposition already stood on Holling / Walker & Salt / Aubin / Daly without him.
4. **James C. Scott** (2026-05-18): included in cluster 4 *Labour, allocation, and what economies actually do* — Scott's legibility-as-active-power sits with Polanyi / Graeber / Sahlins / Waring on the shared theme of *what economies make legible and what they make invisible*.
5. **DP cluster placement**: kept as its own meta-theory cluster rather than merged into *Physical grounding*. Register-difference is real and worth marking.
