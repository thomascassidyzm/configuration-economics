# Exergy Terminology — Targeted Edits

From Lens 4 (physics-rigour) review: the single highest-leverage physics fix in the work. Thread *exergy* through in the handful of places where "energy" is doing precise thermodynamic work. Keep the colloquial "energy" in most prose — accessibility is important, and it reads naturally.

Three levels of edit below, in ascending intrusiveness. You can accept any subset.

---

## Level 1: Add the clarifying proposition (minimal intrusion)

Accept `exergy-not-energy` from `proposed-propositions.md`. This single addition lets every other use of "energy" in the essay stand — a footnote-grade clarification in propositional form.

Effort: 5 minutes. Payoff: any physics-literate reader can follow the footnote-style clarification and read the rest as intended.

---

## Level 2: Tighten the two load-bearing logic blocks (surgical)

The essay's accessible prose is fine as-is. But the `logic` blocks on two propositions are where precision actually matters. Targeted edits:

### `energy-income-inheritance` (P1)

**Current premise:** `"Fossil fuels are finite energy accumulations formed over geological time."`

**Proposed premise:** `"Fossil fuels are finite concentrations of chemical exergy accumulated over geological time."`

Reason: the precise physical claim is about exergy concentration, not raw energy. The raw-energy framing invites the objection that sunlight deposits more energy daily than fossil use.

### `throughput-cost` (P2)

**Current premises:**
- `"Throughput corresponds to energy dissipation and material transformation."`
- `"Dissipation reduces future availability."`

**Proposed premises:**
- `"Throughput corresponds to exergy destruction and material transformation."`
- `"Exergy destruction reduces the available work remaining in the system."`

Reason: "dissipation" is imprecise; "exergy destruction" is the operative thermodynamic quantity.

### `viable-objective` (P22)

**Current claim:** `"A viable economic objective under physical constraint is the maximisation of durable flourishing per unit of bounded throughput."`

No change needed — "throughput" is the right term here (already precise in the Georgescu-Roegen / Daly sense).

**Current premise:** `"Energy income is bounded."`

**Proposed premise:** `"Exergy influx to the economic system is bounded by available flows and sinks."`

Reason: "energy income" reads colloquially but the constraint is really on flux + sink capacity.

## Level 3: Essay-level prose pass (more intrusive, optional)

If you want to go further, a single read-through with these rules:

1. **Keep "energy"** anywhere it reads naturally in accessible prose. Do not replace universally.
2. **Change to "exergy"** only where (a) the sentence is making a physical claim that requires precision, OR (b) a physics-literate reader would misread "energy" as referring to a conserved quantity.
3. **Avoid introducing "exergy" before it's first defined.** First mention should be in §1 (`physical-envelope`) with a parenthetical gloss.

Candidate sentences to consider changing (search-and-consider, not mechanical replace):

### §1 `physical-envelope`
> "Energy flows are bounded."

Could become:
> "Usable energy flows (exergy) are bounded. Total energy is conserved, but the availability of that energy to do work is not."

Or simpler:
> "The exergy available to do work is bounded — not total energy, which is conserved, but the portion that can actually drive processes."

### §2 `accounting-error`
> "...the treatment of **energy savings as income**."

This reads well as-is. Don't change the metaphor; it's doing pedagogic work. Let the new `exergy-not-energy` proposition handle the precision.

### §3 `throughput-proxy`
> "More energy consumed implied more work done."

This is almost the right sentence — "energy consumed" is specifically what a physicist would want rendered as "exergy destroyed." But the accessible phrasing is part of the intentional gentle voice. A footnote or link to `exergy-not-energy` would carry the weight.

### §4 `configuration-value`
> "...can compound without proportional energy increase..."

Consider:
> "...can compound without proportional increase in exergy throughput..."

This is a load-bearing claim (Lens 2 flagged it as unsupported by any proposition). "Energy increase" is genuinely ambiguous here; "exergy throughput" would sharpen.

## Recommendation

- **Minimum:** promote `exergy-not-energy` proposition. 5 minutes; solves the category of objection.
- **Better:** minimum + Level 2 surgical edits. 15 minutes; tightens the propositions that matter formally.
- **Completionist:** all three. 30–45 minutes; makes the essay physics-literate-proof.

The editorial instinct behind the essay's voice is right — accessibility matters. Exergy-through-footnote preserves voice; exergy-in-logic-blocks preserves precision. You don't have to choose between them.
